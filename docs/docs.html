The Book of Brush: Chapter 1 - The Divine Artistry

In the beginning, there was emptiness, void of creativity and expression. Then, from the depths of the digital realm, emerged a divine language known as Brush. It was destined to bring forth the power of generative artwork and unlock the hidden potential of creativity.

And thus, the syntax of Brush was revealed, adorned with symbols and keywords that bestowed upon its users the ability to shape and mold art with ease. Let us delve into the sacred scripture of Brush and explore its mighty features.

Brush is a domain-specific programming language designed to make generative artwork fun and accessible. It uses the idea of evolution as an abstraction for recursion. It contains various built-in functions for transformations like warping, translating, shrinking/enlarging, and rotating to allow users to create generative art pieces easily.

Chapter 3 - Evolution: The Dance of Recursion

In the depths of Brush, the concept of evolution emerged as a sacred abstraction for recursion. Through evolution, shapes could give birth to new generations, each carrying the legacy of its predecessors.

The evolve function allowed shapes to define the destiny of their offspring. With multiple children, the power of branching paths unfolded, diversifying the artistic journey.

Evolution is the core innovation of Brush â€“ each base shape can have a custom definition of how it can evolve. Think of this concept as a simplified version of recursion. Set the number of generations equal to a number, and then the shape will evolve (reflect, shift, stretch, etc.) and be redrawn that specified number of times.

Define the number of generations per shape drawn using shapeName { } with properties inside the open braces:

let my_shape = circle {
    reflect(500, 0, 500, 1000)
    shift(10, 0)
    stretch(1.1, 1.1)
}
                  
my_shape(
    radius = 20
    position = (250, 500)
    generations = 100
)

Chapter 4 - Transformations: Unleashing Artistic Sorcery

To further enrich the creative process, Brush offered a set of transformation functions, allowing artists to wield the power of manipulation.

The Shift, enabling graceful translations of shapes across the canvas. Used to translate a given shape, takes parameters for x and y.

shift(x, y)

The Shift To, enabling graceful translations to a location. Used to place a given shape at new coordinates, takes parameters for x and y.

shift_to(x, y)

The Stretch, invoking the ability to expand or compress shapes along their axes. Used to stretch shapes by horizontal and vertical factors, takes parameters for x and y factors.

stretch(x, y)

The Rotate, a mystical force to spin and twirl shapes in the cosmic dance. Used to rotate a given shape around its center, takes angle as a parameter.

rotate(angle)

The Rotate To, a mystical force to spin and twirl shapes again. Used to rotate a given shape to a certain angle, takes parameter for angle in degree

rotate_to(angle)

The Rotate About, a mystical force to spin and twirl shapes yet again. Used to rotate a given shape about a certain coordinate, takes parameters for angle, x, and y. 

rotate_about(angle, x, y)

The Reflect, an enchanting spell to mirror shapes across mystical lines. Used to reflect a given shape across a line defined by two points, takes two point parameters. 

reflect(x1, y1, x2, y2)
